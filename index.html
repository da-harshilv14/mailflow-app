What it does

Smart filtering, zero configuration

Auto-archive noise

Surface what matters

Interviews, invoices, contest results, and action-required emails are starred and flagged important.

Organised labels

How it works

Three steps, then hands-free

1

Connect your Gmail account

Sign in with Google. MailFlow requests only the gmail.modify scope — enough to read, label, and archive. It never requests permission to delete emails or send on your behalf.

2

Rules run against your inbox

The script fetches your latest emails and checks each one against a set of rules — sender, subject line, and body keywords. Matched emails are labelled and archived. Unmatched emails are checked for importance signals.

3

See only what needs your attention

Your inbox shows the emails that actually matter. Everything else is filed away in the right label, still accessible any time from the Gmail sidebar.

4

Optionally, schedule it

Run the script manually whenever you like, or set up a cron job to keep your inbox continuously clean without any effort on your part.

Permissions

Exactly what's needed, nothing more

Scope requested: gmail.modify

MailFlow uses a single OAuth scope. Here is a precise breakdown of what that allows and what it does not.

Read emails
Fetch message headers (From, Subject, Date) and body snippets to evaluate filter rules.
Add and remove labels
Apply labels like "Shopping/Amazon" or "Security & OTPs", and remove the INBOX label to archive.
Mark as read / Mark as important
Remove the UNREAD label on noise emails; add IMPORTANT and STARRED to emails you should see.
Create labels
Automatically create new labels (e.g. "Shopping/Myntra") the first time a rule fires, if they don't already exist.
